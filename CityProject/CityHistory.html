A Brief History of Wapokeneta

During the native american genocide...


The French built a fort east of the site of Wapakoneta in 1748, called Fort Au Glaize. It was abandoned after the Battle of Fallen Timbers in 1794.
Although Britain took over this territory after the French ceded it following defeat in the Seven Years War, neither they nor the later Americans (after independence) built a succeeding fort here.
Wapakoneta was named as the seat of Auglaize County in 1848.
